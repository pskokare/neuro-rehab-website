In the journey of recovery from a major injury, surgery, or neurological condition, we often focus on exercise and medicine. Yet, one of the most powerful tools for healing is already on your plate. Food is fuel, and when your body is fighting to repair and rebuild, its nutritional needs are dramatically different and significantly greater. A generic "healthy diet" is simply not enough.

Disease-related malnutrition is a major, often overlooked, problem. Studies have shown that up to 50% of patients are already malnourished at the time of their hospital admission, which can significantly delay healing and weaken the immune system.

At Apricot Care Assisted Living and Rehabilitation in Pune, we practice Medical Nutrition Therapy—the science of using targeted nutrition to manage medical conditions, accelerate healing, and optimize your health. Our centeral dietitians are integral members of your rehabilitation team, working to create a personalized, evidence-based nutrition plan that supports your specific recovery goals.

We go beyond simple meal plans. We assess your unique medical needs, manage treatment side effects, and provide your body with the precise building blocks it needs to repair tissue, fight infection, and fuel your recovery from the inside out.

Our Nutrition Therapy Program Is Designed To:

  • Accelerate healing and reduce recovery time after surgery, injury, or illness.
  • Maintain muscle mass and strength during periods of bed rest or treatment.
  • Boost your immune system to help prevent complications and infections.
  • Manage the nutritional impact of treatment side effects like poor appetite or nausea.
  • Improve your energy levels, helping you participate more fully in your rehabilitation.

Why Specialized Nutrition Therapy is Essential for Recovery

Our centeral nutrition services are a vital component of care for individuals facing a wide range of health challenges.

  • Neurological Recovery (Stroke, SCI, TBI): Proper nutrition is critical for brain healing (neuroplasticity). We also help manage common issues like weight changes due to immobility, neurogenic bowel, and provide nutrients essential for preventing pressure sores by maintaining skin health.
  • Oncology Care (During & After Treatment): We help you maintain strength and weight during chemotherapy and radiation. Our dietitians provide expert strategies to manage side effects like taste changes, mouth sores, and nausea, ensuring your body gets the nourishment it needs to tolerate treatment. (Learn more on our Oncology Care Page.
  • Post-Operative & Trauma Healing: After surgery or a major injury, your body's demand for protein, vitamins, and minerals skyrockets. We design a plan rich in these essential nutrients to promote rapid tissue repair, wound healing, and a strong immune response.
  • Orthopedic Recovery: The right nutrition can help manage inflammation and provide the key nutrients (like calcium and vitamin D) needed for strong bone healing after a fracture.
  • Senior Health & Geriatric Care: We focus on combating age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia) and preventing malnutrition in older adults, which is crucial for maintaining strength, energy, and independence.

Our Comprehensive Approach to Nutrition Therapy

Your journey with our centeral dietitian follows a structured, scientific, and deeply personal process.

1

Detailed Nutritional Assessment

Your first session involves a deep dive into your unique situation. We don’t use generic templates. Our dietitian will review your medical history, analyze any recent lab results, discuss your current diet and lifestyle, and understand your personal food preferences and challenges.

  • How we help: Our Physiotherapists conduct a thorough centeral swallow evaluation to determine where the difficulty lies. Treatment involves specific exercises to strengthen the swallowing muscles (like the Mendelsohn Maneuver or Shaker Exercise), strategies to make swallowing safer, and close collaboration with our Nutrition Therapy team to recommend appropriate texture-modified foods and thickened liquids.
2

Personalized & Therapeutic Meal Planning

Based on your assessment, we create a completely personalized nutrition plan. This isn't just a list of "good" and "bad" foods. It is a structured plan that specifies your required intake of calories, protein, fats, and micronutrients, designed to achieve a specific therapeutic outcome. We provide sample meal plans and recipes to make following the plan easy and enjoyable.

3

Specialized Support for Swallowing Difficulties (Dysphagia)

For many of our neuro patients, swallowing can be a challenge. Our dietitian works closely with our Speech & Swallow Physiotherapists to design diets that are both safe and nutritious.

  • Texture Modification: We provide expert guidance on preparing texture-modified foods (e.g., pureed, minced & moist) that are easier and safer to swallow.
  • Thickened Liquids: We help you implement plans for thickened liquids to prevent aspiration (liquids entering the lungs) while ensuring proper hydration.
4

Expert Management of Tube Feeding (Enteral Nutrition)

For patients who cannot eat orally, our centeral dietitians are experts in managing enteral nutrition.

  • Formula Calculation: We calculate the precise type and amount of tube feeding formula needed to meet 100% of your nutritional requirements.
  • Monitoring & Adjustment: We continuously monitor your tolerance and progress, adjusting the plan as needed to support your recovery and coordinate with your medical team.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between a Dietitian and a Nutritionist?

A Registered Dietitian (RD) is a board-certified food and nutrition expert who has met strict academic and professional requirements. They are qualified to practice Medical Nutrition Therapy and treat centeral conditions. The term "nutritionist" is less regulated. At sifoxen Care, our services are provided by qualified centeral dietitians.

My appetite is very poor because of my medical treatment. How can you help?

This is a very common and critical challenge. Our dietitian will work with you to find solutions. This may include smaller, more frequent meals; nutrient-dense smoothies and shakes; foods that are easier to eat; or strategies to manage side effects like nausea that may be affecting your appetite.

Will I have to eat boring or tasteless food?

Absolutely not. Our philosophy is that food should be enjoyed. We work with you and your family to find creative ways to make your therapeutic diet delicious and satisfying. We provide recipes and suggestions to prove that healthy, healing food can also be great food.

Does this service include weight loss plans?

While weight management (either loss or gain) can be a component of a therapeutic plan—for instance, to reduce stress on joints or rebuild body mass after illness—our primary focus is on Medical Nutrition Therapy. We design diets to treat and manage specific medical conditions and optimize healing, which is different from a standard commercial weight loss program.
