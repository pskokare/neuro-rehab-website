Standards & Rights

Did You Know?

Neurorehabilitation Standards and Rights of the Patient

Did you hear that India's national healthcare policy emphasizes the holistic, team-oriented neurorehabilitative approach with rights and accessibility at its center?The recent national recommendations and market research statistics indicate that neurorehabilitation is not only a physical recovery process, but it should also consider emotional health, social involvement, and autonomy over the long term.

The government's recommendations are based on the Rehabilitation 2030 strategy of the World Health Organization: big hospitals are considered the major facilities and will provide an infrastructure of community clinics and tele-rehabilitation stations. This implies that patients in rural or remote settings have the right to continuous professional rehabilitation and single-time treatment. The implementation of digital technology, such as supervised home care and virtual therapy, facilitates continuity, progress monitoring, and personalized treatment, despite geography or mobility.

National laws like the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act (2016) & Mental Health Care Act (2017) guarantee every patient the right to comprehensive rehabilitation, informed decision-making, and full participation in their own care journey.
