A Team That Treats the Nervous System, Not Just the Symptom

Most clinics treat what they can see: the limp, the stiff arm, the poor balance. Our team goes deeper. We are trained to understand why those symptoms exist at a neurological level, and we build your treatment around correcting the root cause, not just managing the surface presentation.

This means your sessions are designed around neuroplasticity, the brain and nervous system's proven ability to rewire, adapt, and recover, rather than simply stretching a tight muscle or applying a machine to a sore area. How Our Neuro Physiotherapy Doctors in Pune Approach Your Recovery

Recovery from a neurological condition is not linear, and no two patients follow the same path. Our approach begins with a thorough clinical assessment that maps your current neurological function, movement ability, muscle tone, balance, coordination, and daily activity limitations. From there, your neurophysiotherapist builds a structured, personalised treatment plan with clear milestones and goals.

We use internationally recognised neurological rehabilitation methods including task-specific training, proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation (PNF), Bobath and NDT techniques, gait retraining, and progressive functional exercise. Every technique we use is backed by clinical evidence and adapted specifically to your condition and recovery stage.

We also believe recovery does not end when your session does. Your neurophysiotherapist will design a home programme, train your caregiver if needed, and track your progress closely between visits to keep your recovery moving forward at every stage.

Your First Visit

What Happens in a Neuro Physiotherapy Session at Our Pune Clinic

Many patients and their families are unsure what to expect from their first neuro physiotherapy session. Here is what a typical visit at Apricot Care looks like.

1

Detailed Assessment

Your first session is a comprehensive neurological and functional evaluation. Your therapist will assess your movement patterns, strength, tone, reflexes, balance, sensation, and how your condition currently affects your daily life. This gives us the full picture before we begin treatment.

2

Goal Setting

Based on your assessment, your therapist will sit with you and your family to define clear, achievable goals, whether that's walking independently, using your affected arm again, reducing fall risk, or returning to work.

3

Hands-On Treatment

Every session combines manual therapy techniques, active movement retraining, and targeted exercises. Your therapist works directly with you throughout: no machines running unsupervised, no assistants taking over.

4

Home Programme

At the end of each session, you receive guidance on exercises and activities to continue at home. This is critical: the progress you make between sessions is just as important as the work done in the clinic.

5

Progress Review

Your treatment plan is regularly reviewed and updated as you improve. We adjust your programme as your nervous system responds and your function advances.

Clinical Specialisations

Conditions We Specialise In — Our Neuro Physiotherapy Clinic Near You

Our neurophysiotherapy team has deep clinical experience across the full range of neurological conditions. If you or someone in your family has been diagnosed with any of the following, our team is equipped and ready to help.

Stroke & Hemiplegia Rehabilitation

Stroke is one of the most common reasons patients seek neuro physiotherapy in Pune. Whether you experienced a stroke weeks, months, or years ago, our team can help you regain movement in your affected arm and leg, improve your walking pattern, reduce spasticity, and rebuild functional independence for daily activities. Early intervention gives the best results, but it is never too late to start.

Parkinson's Disease Physiotherapy

Parkinson's disease progressively affects movement, balance, and coordination. Our physiotherapy programme for Parkinson's patients focuses on maintaining mobility for as long as possible, improving walking safety, reducing the risk of falls, managing freezing episodes, and preserving quality of life through targeted exercise and movement strategies.

Spinal Cord Injury Recovery

A spinal cord injury can be devastating, but the right rehabilitation programme makes a profound difference in what a patient is able to achieve. Our team works with both complete and incomplete spinal cord injuries, focusing on maximising remaining function, improving transfers and posture, developing upper limb strength, and building the greatest possible degree of independence.

Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) Rehabilitation

Recovering from a traumatic brain injury requires patience, precision, and a therapist who understands the complex and unpredictable nature of brain recovery. We focus on rebuilding motor control, coordination, and functional strength, helping patients regain the ability to perform everyday tasks and move safely in their environment.

Cerebral Palsy Management Across All Ages

Cerebral palsy affects movement, muscle tone, and posture, and the right physiotherapy support at every stage of life makes a significant difference. We work with children, adolescents, and adults living with cerebral palsy to improve movement quality, manage spasticity, enhance posture, and support greater participation in daily life and activities.

Multiple Sclerosis — Maintaining Mobility and Independence

MS is an unpredictable, progressive condition, and physiotherapy plays a vital role in managing its impact. Our approach focuses on reducing fatigue, improving balance and walking ability, maintaining strength, and empowering patients with strategies to stay as active and independent as possible through every phase of the condition.

Guillain-Barré Syndrome Recovery

GBS can cause rapid, severe weakness across the body, but with the right rehabilitation, most patients make significant recoveries. Our programme focuses on progressive strength restoration, endurance rebuilding, sensory retraining, and carefully graded functional reactivation as your nervous system recovers.

Peripheral Neuropathy and Nerve Damage Physiotherapy

Peripheral neuropathy causes weakness, numbness, tingling, and balance problems that significantly impact daily life. Our team addresses these through targeted strengthening exercises, balance and proprioception retraining, sensory stimulation techniques, and education on managing symptoms safely at home.
