We Specialize in Rehabilitation For All Major Surgeries

Our Physiotherapists in Pune have deep, protocol-based expertise in providing post-operative care, with a special focus on orthopedic procedures.

    We Specialize in Rehabilitation For All Major Surgeries

  • Total Knee Replacement (TKR): Our primary focus is on regaining full knee extension (straightening) and flexion (bending), controlling swelling, and rebuilding quadriceps strength to ensure a stable, pain-free knee.

  • Spine Surgeries & Fracture Repair: Our expert team guides you through core stabilization and safe, progressive weight-bearing to ensure optimal healing.

Your Recovery, The Four Phases: Our Structured Approach

Every recovery is personal, but it must follow a structured, evidence-based path. Our Physiotherapists guide you meticulously through each of these four distinct phases, which is particularly critical for TKR and THR recovery.

1

The Protective Phase (Immediately Post-Op)

This initial phase is all about protecting the new joint and managing the body's natural response to surgery.

  • Our Focus: We prioritize controlling your pain and swelling using cryotherapy and compression. For TKR, achieving full knee extension early is a critical goal we work on from day one. Your therapist will use gentle passive or active-assisted motion to prevent your joint from getting stiff, without stressing the implant. We also ensure you are safe and confident using a walker or crutches.
2

The Mobility & Activation Phase

As the initial healing progresses, the focus shifts to actively restoring your movement and re-engaging your muscles.

  • Our Focus: You will begin to move the joint through its full range of motion under your own power. We introduce gentle muscle activation exercises for the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. For THR patients, we rigorously enforce and practice hip precautions during all movements.
3

The Strengthening & Functional Phase

This is where you build the solid foundation of strength needed to support your new joint for a lifetime.

  • Our Focus: We design a program of progressive resistive exercises to build robust strength. We then translate that strength into real-world, functional movements like getting up from a chair without using your hands, navigating stairs with confidence, and walking with a normal gait pattern.
4

The Return to Life Phase

The final phase is about returning to every aspect of your life with total confidence in your new joint.

  • Our Focus: We tailor this phase to your personal goals, whether it's returning to walking for an hour, gardening, or playing a sport. We provide you with a long-term home exercise program to ensure your results last a lifetime.

Frequently Asked Questions

My surgeon gave me a list of exercises. Why do I need a physiotherapist?

A list of exercises is a great start, but it's not a substitute for expert care. A specialist physiotherapist ensures you perform exercises with perfect form, uses hands-on manual therapy to improve mobility and reduce pain in ways exercises alone cannot, and progresses you at the appropriate rate based on your specific healing. This personalized guidance is the key to a faster, safer, and more complete recovery.

When is the best time to schedule my first appointment for TKR/THR rehab?

The ideal time is before your surgery. A "pre-hab" appointment allows us to perform a baseline assessment, teach you how to use a walker or crutches, and schedule your first post-op sessions in advance. This ensures there is absolutely no delay in starting your critical recovery process the moment your surgeon gives you the green light.

How long will my recovery take after a joint replacement?

While you will be up and walking within a day or two of surgery, a full recovery is a marathon, not a sprint. You can expect to be in a formal rehabilitation program for at least 3 months. However, regaining your full strength and function can take anywhere from 6 months to a full year of consistent effort.
