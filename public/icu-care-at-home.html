When is ICU at Home the Right Choice?

Our ICU at Home service is the ideal solution for patients who are medically stable but require a level of care that cannot be managed by family or a standard nurse. This includes:

  • Long-Term Ventilator Support: For patients who are dependent on mechanical ventilation to breathe.
  • Post-Discharge Critical Care: For patients discharged from the hospital after major surgery, stroke, or trauma who still require intensive monitoring.
  • Advanced Neurological Conditions: For individuals with conditions like late-stage ALS/MND or high-level spinal cord injuries who have complex respiratory needs.
  • Tracheostomy & Feeding Tube Management: For patients who require expert, 24/7 management of these devices.

One of the most powerful reasons to choose home-based care is safety. Hospital-Acquired Infections (HAIs) are a serious risk, with studies indicating they are a significant cause of complications in prolonged hospital stays. Our service provides one-on-one nursing in a controlled home environment, dramatically reducing this risk.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is it really as safe as a hospital ICU?

For patients who are medically stable but require long-term intensive support (like ventilation), our ICU at Home service is a safe and often preferred alternative. We use the same equipment and adhere to strict centeral protocols, with the added benefit of dedicated one-on-one nursing, which can be superior to the staff-to-patient ratio in a busy hospital.

How do you handle emergencies like a power outage?

This is a critical part of our setup protocol. Our team ensures that all essential life-support equipment (like the ventilator and monitor) is connected to a robust power backup system (like an inverter and generator backup) that is tested and reliable, ensuring uninterrupted care.

Can physiotherapy and other therapies really be done in a home ICU setting?

Yes, absolutely. In fact, it's a key part of the recovery process. Our Physiotherapists and respiratory Physiotherapists are trained to provide essential therapy at the bedside, helping to maintain joint mobility, prevent muscle wasting, and support the weaning process from the ventilator.

Is this service more affordable than a hospital ICU?

For long-term stays, our ICU at Home service is often significantly more cost-effective than a private hospital ICU. It eliminates many of the overhead costs of a hospital while providing a higher ratio of dedicated nursing care.
