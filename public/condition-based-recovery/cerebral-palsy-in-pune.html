Understanding Cerebral Palsy in Adulthood

Cerebral Palsy is a group of disorders affecting a person's ability to move and maintain balance and posture. It is caused by damage to the developing brain, which most often occurs before birth. It's important to remember that CP is non-progressive—the original brain injury does not get worse over time.

However, the strain of living with abnormal muscle tone and movement patterns for many years can lead to secondary musculoskeletal conditions in adulthood. With Cerebral Palsy being one of the most common causes of childhood disability in India, there is a growing community of adults who require specialized care to manage these long-term effects.

Why Do Needs Change in Adulthood?

As an adult with CP, you may start to experience new challenges, including:

  • Chronic Pain: From years of stress on joints and muscles.
  • Increased Fatigue: The energy required to move can be significantly higher, leading to exhaustion.
  • Premature Aging: The physical demands of CP can lead to musculoskeletal issues that are more common in an older population.
  • Changes in Mobility: A walking pattern that worked in your youth may become less efficient or more painful over time.

Our program is specifically designed to proactively address these adult-onset challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

I've had Cerebral Palsy my whole life and had a lot of therapy as a child. Why do I need it now?

That's an excellent question. While you are an expert on your own body, therapy for an adult with CP is very different. It's not about learning to walk for the first time; it's about proactively managing the secondary effects of aging with CP, such as pain and fatigue. It's about preserving the function you have and preventing future problems, which requires a new set of strategies.

I'm finding that walking is more tiring and painful than it used to be. Is this normal?

this is a very common experience for adults with CP. Over time, the high energy cost of walking with an altered gait pattern, combined with potential joint changes, can lead to increased pain and fatigue. Our gait analysis and functional strengthening programs are designed to address this directly by improving your efficiency and reducing strain.

What can be done about the chronic muscle and joint pain I experience?

A great deal can be done. Our Physiotherapists use a combination of hands-on manual therapy to release muscle tension, specific stretching programs to improve flexibility, and education on proper body mechanics and joint protection strategies. Our goal is to give you a comprehensive toolkit to manage and reduce your pain.

How is therapy for an adult with CP different from therapy for a child?

Therapy for children with CP is developmental and focused on helping them reach key milestones for the first time. Therapy for adults is focused on management, preservation, and prevention. We address age-related changes, manage chronic pain, and focus on strategies that will allow you to maintain your hard-earned independence for as long as possible. The goals and the approach are much more focused on long-term wellness.
