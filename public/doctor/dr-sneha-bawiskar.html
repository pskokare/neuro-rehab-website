About

Dr. Sneha Bawiskar

Dr. Sneha Bawiskar is a Neuro Physiotherapist with 3 years of clinical experience and currently serves as the OPD Head at Apricot Care Physiotherapy, Kharadi, Pune. She specialises in handling neurological cases such as stroke, Parkinson’s disease, Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS), and spinal cord injury (SCI).

She completed her post-graduation training at KEM Hospital, Mumbai, and her undergraduate physiotherapy from K.J. Somaiya College of Physiotherapy. Her clinical approach focuses on treating adult neurological cases holistically, with strong exposure to acute case management and ICU-based rehabilitation planning. She supports patients through structured therapy routines that improve mobility, balance, confidence in movement, and functional independence. Caregivers are also guided with simple home routines to maintain continuity between sessions.

  • Neuro Rehabilitation: Supports recovery in neurological conditions through structured therapy aimed at improving movement control, balance, and independence.
  • Mobility and Balance Training: Works on stability, posture control, and safe movement for standing, transfers, and daily activities.
  • OPD Rehabilitation Planning: Helps patients follow a clear rehab pathway in an OPD setup, with regular tracking and upgrades in the therapy plan.
  • Group Therapy Sessions: Conducts guided group therapies that encourage consistent practice, motivation, and functional improvements in a supportive environment.

What to Expect in Your Rehab Plan
Assessment and Goal Setting

A focused review of movement, balance, strength, coordination, and daily activity limitations, followed by clear therapy goals.

Personalised Therapy Plan

A plan based on what the patient can do today and what needs to improve next, with functional practice included for daily movement and independence.

Step-by-step Progression

Therapy is progressed safely based on tolerance and practical milestones like transfers, standing, walking, endurance, and daily task performance.

Home Guidance and Caregiver Support

Simple routines are shared so progress continues between sessions. Caregivers are guided on safe support methods when needed.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How soon should rehabilitation start after a stroke?

It depends on medical stability and your doctor’s advice. The first visit usually focuses on assessment and safe goal setting.

How many sessions will I need?

It varies by condition and severity. A realistic plan is shared after evaluation.

Do you provide a home exercise plan?

Yes, home guidance is shared so progress continues between sessions.

Do you handle acute neurological cases?

Yes, she has exposure to acute case handling and ICU-based rehabilitation planning as part of her neuro training.

Do you guarantee recovery?

Recovery differs for every patient. The focus stays on safe care, measurable improvement, and steady progress.
