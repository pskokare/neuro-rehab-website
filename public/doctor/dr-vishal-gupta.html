About

Dr. Vishal Gupta

Dr. Vishal Gupta is a Clinical Physiotherapist at Apricot Care Physiotherapy and Wellness, Kharadi, Pune. He completed his Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPTh) from Dhole Patil College of Physiotherapy, Pune, and has supplemented his academic training with an internationally recognised certification in Clinical Neurodynamic Solutions (NDS) from Australia.

At Apricot Care, Dr. Vishal works with adult neuro patients and musculoskeletal cases, treating conditions including stroke, spinal cord injuries, and post-surgical neurological presentations. His clinical focus is on improving mobility, balance, and functional independence through personalised, evidence-based therapy programs. His approach is structured around each patient's current ability, with clear rehabilitation goals and practical progression milestones built into every plan.

  • Adult Neuro Rehabilitation: Works with adult patients managing neurological conditions to improve strength, balance, coordination, mobility, and independence in daily activities.
  • Musculoskeletal Injury Management: Assesses and manages musculoskeletal injuries through targeted physiotherapy, restoring movement, strength, and function safely.
  • Stroke Rehabilitation: Builds step-by-step recovery plans post-stroke to improve mobility, balance, walking ability, and functional independence over time.
  • Spinal Cord Injury Rehabilitation: Supports structured rehabilitation for spinal cord injury patients, focusing on safe mobility training and practical functional improvements.
  • Balance Training and Gait Re-education: Works on stability, posture control, walking pattern correction, and endurance based on the patient's current recovery stage and functional capacity.
  • Post-Surgical Neurological Recovery: Provides rehabilitation support following neurological surgery to restore movement, confidence, and function safely.
  • Functional Independence Training: Task-specific training designed to help patients perform daily activities with greater ease, control, and confidence.
  • Neuromuscular Re-education: Targets movement control and coordination so patients can perform daily tasks with better muscle activation and less compensatory effort.
  • Clinical Neurodynamics (NDS): Applies evidence-based neurodynamic assessment and treatment techniques to address nerve mobility restrictions and improve sensorimotor function.

What to Expect in Your Rehab Plan
1) Assessment and Goal Setting

A focused assessment of mobility, balance, strength, nerve function, and functional limitations, followed by clear and realistic rehabilitation goals agreed upon with the patient.

2) Personalised Therapy Plan

A plan built around what the patient can do today and what needs to improve next, combining evidence-based neuro physiotherapy with neurodynamic techniques and functional training matched to current ability.

3) Step-by-step Progression

Therapy is progressed safely based on the patient's tolerance, fatigue levels, and practical milestones such as sitting balance, standing stability, walking, and daily task performance.

4) Home Guidance and Caregiver Support

Simple home exercise routines are shared to support continuity between sessions. Caregivers are guided on safe assistance methods when required.

Frequently Asked Questions

What conditions does Dr. Vishal Gupta treat?

He works with adult neuro patients and musculoskeletal cases, including stroke, spinal cord injuries, post-surgical neurological recovery, balance issues, and movement restrictions.

What is Clinical Neurodynamics (NDS)?

Clinical Neurodynamics is an evidence-based approach to assessing and treating nerve mobility restrictions. Dr. Vishal holds a certification in NDS from Australia, which he applies to improve sensorimotor function and movement quality.

How soon should rehabilitation start after a stroke?

It depends on medical stability and the treating doctor's advice. The first session typically focuses on assessment and establishing safe, realistic rehabilitation goals.

How many sessions will I need?

The number of sessions depends on the condition, its severity, and individual recovery progress. A realistic plan is discussed after the initial assessment.

Do you provide a home exercise plan?

Yes, practical home routines are shared after each phase of therapy to support steady progress between clinic sessions.
